History & Culture

Türkiye is a transcontinental nation with a legacy spanning thousands of years. It was the heart of the Byzantine and Ottoman Empires before becoming a modern, secular republic in 1923 under the leadership of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk.

Today, it is known for its "Turkish tea" culture, world-class cuisine (especially its diverse breads and kebabs), and the historic "Turkish stone" or turquoise.

Top Tourist Attractions

  • Istanbul: Home to the iconic Hagia Sophia and the bustling Grand Bazaar.
  • Cappadocia: Famous for its "fairy chimney" rock formations and magical sunrise hot air balloon rides.
  • Pamukkale: Known as the "Cotton Castle," featuring white travertine terraces filled with mineral-rich thermal waters.
  • Ephesus: One of the most complete ancient Greco-Roman cities in the Mediterranean.
