History & Culture

France is one of the oldest nations in the world, tracing its roots back to the Gauls and later the Franks, from whom it takes its name. It reached heights of power under Louis XIV and was transformed by the French Revolution of 1789, which introduced the principles of liberty and equality.

It is globally renowned for its haute couture, philosophical Enlightenment legacy, and a culinary tradition that includes over 1,500 varieties of cheese.

Top Tourist Attractions

  • Eiffel Tower: The global symbol of France, located in the heart of Paris.
  • The Louvre Museum: The world's largest and most visited art museum, housing the Mona Lisa.
  • Palace of Versailles: A masterpiece of 17th-century French art and a former seat of royal power.
  • Mont Saint-Michel: A stunning medieval abbey perched on a rocky islet in Normandy.
  • French Riviera (Côte d'Azur): Famous for its glamorous beaches and cities like Nice and Cannes.
