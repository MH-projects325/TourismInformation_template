History & Culture

Spain's history is a rich tapestry of Roman, Visigoth, and Moorish influences. The Reconquista led to the unification of the country under the Catholic Monarchs in 1492, the same year Christopher Columbus reached the Americas.

Spanish culture is vibrant and diverse, famous for its Flamenco music, world-class football, and the tradition of tapas. It is also home to incredible architecture ranging from Roman aqueducts to the surreal modernism of Antoni Gaudí.

Top Tourist Attractions

  • Sagrada Família (Barcelona): Gaudí’s unfinished masterpiece and one of the most visited churches in the world.
  • The Alhambra (Granada): A stunning Moorish palace and fortress complex.
  • Prado Museum (Madrid): One of the world's premier art galleries.
  • Seville Cathedral: The largest Gothic cathedral in the world.
  • Park Güell (Barcelona): A whimsical public park filled with colorful mosaics.
